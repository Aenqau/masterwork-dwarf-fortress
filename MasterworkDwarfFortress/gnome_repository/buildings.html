A big amount of new reactions can be done here. You can extract new kinds of metal strands from metals bars, enabling silver, golden, mithril or orichalcum clothing. You can craft single instruments or crafts from boulders, make rope from leather. Illumination, like braziers, candelabras and fireplaces can be created.

The crafts section for bone and shell contain all armor items you can produce.

Dye, Blood, Graphite and Ash can be made into ink. You need 3 pots.

Many new sorts of food are available. Roasting inedible seeds make them edible. Mined "living rock" can be butchered for meat, mined "cave fungus" can be made into cheese.

Extract blood from fresh meat, for all kinds of purposes from sacrifices to dark magic.

Boil horn/hoof materials to create glue. Glue is needed for binding books and the making of plywood; other sources include spore-trees or bloated tubers.

reaction_tanner.txt

TANNING: Adds specific tanning reactions for each animal skin type. You can tan skin, tough skin, chitin, shell and scales into an amount of leather based on the creatures size. Usually around 2-4 pieces, smaller creatures might give none, while massive monsters give dozens.

LEATHER: Normal leather can be used for suede(3) or vellum(3) from one piece. Suede is very thin leather, useless for combat, but good for backpacks or quivers. Vellum is used for the library system.

FUR/STOMACH/GUT/HORN: Tan stomachs directly into a waterskins, rope from guts/intestines and pelts into a furs. A fur is the very same as leather, but also protects from cold. Fur is needed for glacier embarks. If you dont want the fur, you can shave of the hairs to create a piece of leather and a unit of hair thread. Horns can be shaped into a small dagger, for eariest militias.

FEATHERS: Birds leave feathers upon being butchered.

Material strength:

Leather = Vanilla Leather. ::: Tough leather = Copper.

Studded leather = Bronze. ::: Lamellar leather = Iron.

Chitinplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Chitin = Iron, very light.

Scaleplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Scale = Iron, fireproof.

Shellplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Shellplate = Between iron and steel, heavy.

Clay Oven -

Build labor
Skill

Stonecrafting & Pottery

reaction_clay_oven.txt

The vanilla kiln has been splitted into the kiln and the clay oven. The clay oven can make any items from clay, fireclay or kaolinite, as well as a unit of fuel. Clay produces stoneware items, Fireclay makes earthenware items and kaolinite produces porcelain items.

The "collect clay" zones have been renamed to "collect clay/dirt/peat", since more types of soil are valid targets now. Dirt can only be used as free boulders for constructions, while peat can be compressed and burned as low-cost fuel.

reaction_smelter.txt

You can use plywood for one unit of charcoal per log, burn compact peat to 2-4 bars of coke and the very valuable anthracite for 12 bars of coke. Plywood is a byproduct of the wood-industry, compact peat can either be found in soil-layers or produced by compressing peat, and anthrazite is a rare coal cluster found deep underground.

You can create a metal anvil from five bars. You no longer need to bring one at embark.

Smelting ore also creates a waste product called Slag. These bars clutter up your smelter and should be destroyed in the slagpit or used up as buildmats.

building_crematory.txt
reaction_crematory.txt

Great for cleaning up your fort. You can burn all kinds of different items, including body parts and remains, any sort of items and wooden furniture to get some ash or charcoal out of it. Wooden items produce coal for sure, body parts/remains might leave ash, and all smaller items have a small chance for either.

This is mostly done to reduce the amount of items, so it is nor very efficient in creating ash or coal. I advise to build one or two of these next to your refuse piles.

The slag pit helps you clean up waste from metal production. Slag bars can not be used for much and clutter up your furnaces. You can destroy them or use them as base material for either concrete blocks or potash. Lots and lots of potash, which comes in handy with the harder farming system.
